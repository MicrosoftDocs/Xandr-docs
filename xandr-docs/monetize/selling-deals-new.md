# Selling Deals

## Overview

Selling deals allows publishers and sellers to offer inventory directly to selected buyers under negotiated terms, rather than relying solely on open auctions. Deals help you increase control, improve yield, and build strategic buyer relationships for premium inventory.

This section guides you through the complete seller workflow — from negotiation to creation, management, and performance analysis.

---

## Who This Is For

This documentation is intended for:

- **Supply-side sellers / publishers** managing premium inventory  
- **Ad Operations teams** responsible for deal setup and optimization  
- **Revenue managers** monitoring performance and yield  
- **Programmatic strategists** negotiating private marketplace access  

If you are a buyer, see the **Buying Deals** section.

---

## What Is a Deal?

A *deal* is a private agreement between a seller and one or more buyers that defines:

- Inventory access  
- Pricing terms  
- Auction type  
- Targeting constraints  
- Priority relative to open auction traffic  

Deals allow sellers to control demand access while maximizing revenue.

---

## Types of Deals (At a Glance)

| Deal Type | When to Use | Typical Goal |
|------------|-------------|--------------|
| Custom Deal | Direct negotiation with specific buyer | Premium inventory control |
| Curated Deal | Package inventory into themed segments | Strategic demand scaling |
| Auction-Based Deal | Flexible bidding within constraints | Competitive pricing |
| Fixed / Programmatic Guaranteed | Guaranteed delivery at fixed price | Revenue predictability |

## When Should You Use Deals?

Use deals when you want to:

- Offer premium inventory selectively  
- Maintain price floors above open auction  
- Build long-term buyer partnerships  
- Guarantee predictable revenue  
- Increase competition within controlled access  

If your goal is broad monetization without buyer restrictions, open auction may be more appropriate.

---

## Common Seller Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Premium Homepage Takeover

You negotiate a fixed CPM deal with a strategic advertiser for guaranteed homepage inventory.

### Scenario 2: Seasonal Packaging

You bundle travel-related inventory into a curated deal for a demand partner targeting holiday campaigns.

### Scenario 3: Controlled Buyer Testing

You allow select DSPs access to exclusive segments to test yield before expanding broadly.

---

## Key Success Metrics

Measure deal effectiveness using:

- Revenue uplift vs open auction  
- Buyer bid rate  
- Deal win rate  
- Fill rate  
- Average CPM improvement  
- Delivery pacing vs agreement  

Strong deals show consistent buyer participation and stable pricing above open auction benchmarks.
